Causes of Mental Health Problems

Mental health can be affected by many different factors. These causes may be linked to lifestyle, personal experiences, or outside pressures. Different people are affected in different ways.

Young adults may experience stress from education, work, relationships, or financial concerns. If these pressures continue for a long time, they can have a negative effect on mental wellbeing.

Common causes

  • Academic pressure and exams
  • Financial worries
  • Family problems
  • Relationship difficulties
  • Social media pressure
  • Lack of sleep

Understanding the Causes

Understanding the causes of poor mental health can help people recognise problems early and take steps to look after their wellbeing.

Mental health problems rarely have a single cause. In many cases, a combination of biological, psychological, and environmental factors may contribute to changes in a person’s mental wellbeing.

Life events such as moving to a new place, experiencing loss, or facing long-term stress can also influence mental health. When people feel overwhelmed by these situations, it may affect their ability to cope effectively.

Recognising potential causes of stress or emotional pressure can help individuals identify what may be affecting their wellbeing. This awareness may encourage people to make positive changes or seek appropriate support.
